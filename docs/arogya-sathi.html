The problem

Most people don't ignore symptoms because they don't care.

They ignore them because the alternative — a long wait, a confusing search result, a language barrier — feels harder than doing nothing.

＋Limited access to healthcare, especially outside cities
＋Poor or no internet connectivity for large stretches of the day
＋Low awareness of what a symptom actually means
＋Language barriers with existing digital health tools
＋Long clinic waiting times for routine questions
＋Medical information that's hard to read or trust
＋No easy way to track health continuously at home
＋Unreliable, unsourced advice from random online searches

Safety & compliance

Trust is the actual product.

What Arogya Sathi will always do

  • State clearly that it does not diagnose disease — it supports a decision, doctors make it
  • Recommend a qualified healthcare professional whenever a symptom warrants one
  • Flag emergency symptoms — chest pain, severe breathing difficulty, stroke signs — and push toward immediate care
  • Ask for consent before syncing any wearable device, and store health data securely
Where this is headed

Three phases, from prototype to platform.

  • AI chatbot with grounded, sourced answers
  • Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline
  • Symptom checker across 10 common symptoms
  • Health dashboard with simulated wearable data
  • Real wearable integration — Health Connect, Apple Health, Bluetooth devices
  • Medicine reminders with adherence history
  • Family profiles for every household member
  • Telemedicine integration
  • Full nutrition analysis
  • Multilingual voice assistant across regional languages
  • Government health scheme integration
  • Predictive analytics
  • Direct hospital connectivity
